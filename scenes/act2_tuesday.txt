*label act2_tuesday

You wake up to the soft glow of the city outside your window, but the weight of yesterday still lingers. The Monday schedule, your first attempt to structure everyone's work around assumed productivity, left you with mixed results. 

The morning meeting is looming, and you notice a hint of tension as you enter the room. Lina looks drained, rubbing her temples. Jasper is yawning loudly, slouched in his chair, clearly still on night mode. Amara has her notebook open, pen poised, calm as ever, while Theo hums quietly to himself, seemingly unconcerned by the chaos around him.

"Morning," you say, trying to sound upbeat. "Let's get through the updates quickly. I want to see how far we've come."

Lina snaps back more sharply than usual. "I barely had time to finish the first sketches before the sync. I need space in the mornings to think!"

Jasper yawns again. "Maybe if some of us weren't chained to morning meetings, we’d actually get things done."

Amara looks between you and the others, serene but firm. "Perhaps our schedules are misaligned. Productivity isn’t about forcing energy—it’s about timing."

Theo leans forward, curious. "You mean like… body clocks?" His eyes light up. "Everyone has their rhythm, right?"

You nod slowly, realizing he’s right. Yesterday’s attempts to force uniformity have created tension and inefficiency.

Jasper, on the other hand, seems to have a spark in his eyes now that the sun is lowering. His brilliance is undeniable, but it arrives too late to salvage the morning’s work. Amara sits in her corner, steadily progressing but isolated, absorbed in her midday solitude.

Theo sidles up to you. "I think I’ve got a sense of how everyone works," he whispers. "Lina is strongest early, Jasper peaks at night, Amara is steady midday, and I can adapt to anything. Maybe you should consider reassigning tasks?"
